Aesthetic views and proximity                                                            

Aesthetic, recreational and cultural benefits

Viewshed

The benefit resulting from the viewing of of scenic landscapes, such as mountains or water bodies, which is carried across lines of sight and is affected by obstructions or visual blight in between viewers and scenic objects.

Proximity to open space

The benefit resulting from access to nearby open space, such as parks, forests, wetlands, beaches or golf courses, which offers privacy and access to open space.

Recreation

The contribution of ecosystems toward providing diverse outdoor recreational opportunities such as hiking, bird watching, hunting, kayaking, etc.

 

Cultural identity

Certain landscape features and iconic species are important in determining a sense of identity for local groups and may provide community, cultural and spiritual value.

 

Sensory enjoyment, recreational potential and cultural values provided by the landscape are crucial to human well-being. ARIES supports the quantification of all these types of aesthetic value.

In the field

Western Washington State, USA

ARIES Viewshed Analysis is being used to help planners understand the consequences of development on property value through impacts on aesthetic views. Aesthetic view values were computed and used to determine property value. Views of mountains and water bodies provide value to homeowners and municipal tax bases. City and regional land use policies can either improve or degrade these values over time.
Eastern Washington State, USA

ARIES Proximity Analysis is being used to help planners understand the consequences of development on property value through impacts on aesthetic views and open space proximity. Careful open space planning can improve both property values and quality of life for residents.
